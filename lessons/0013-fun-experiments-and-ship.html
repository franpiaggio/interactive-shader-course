Raymarching for Creative Art · Lesson 13 · The send-off

Fun experiments & ship them

No new theory — just proof of how far the toolkit goes. Three showpieces built only from things you've already done. Play with each, read the one idea that makes it tick, then take it to Shadertoy.

How to use this lesson Hit ▶ Run, drag the sliders, and watch what each one changes — that's how you learn what a shader does. The full code sits under each one, ready to paste into Shadertoy. Slider values shown there are already baked in as consts, so it runs unchanged.

1 · Lava lamp — orbiting metaballs

Six spheres drift on sine waves and melt together with smin, raymarched (L4), then lit with diffuse + specular (L7), a cosine palette, and a Fresnel rim (edges glow when you look across them). blend is the magic: low = separate blobs, high = one gooey mass.

2 · Neon tunnel — infinite rings

No raymarching at all — just 2D UVs in polar form. The whole illusion is one line: depth = 0.35/r — dividing by the radius makes the center read as far away (perspective), so concentric rings become a corridor. Add iTime to that depth and the ribs rush toward you — you're flying. speed sets how fast you fly; bend makes the corridor turn — its vanishing point steers around like driving through curves, and the slider sets how sharp the turns get (0 = dead straight). A palette driven by depth recolors the walls as they pass.

3 · Kaleidoscope plasma — pure 2D

Back to 2D UVs: fold the angle into a mirrored wedge (a kaleidoscope), sample fbm noise drifting in time, and color it with a palette. No 3D at all — and still mesmerizing. segments sets the symmetry; scale the swirl detail.
